# GenAI Interview Questions and Mastery Checklist

This page is meant to help you revise the whole module and prepare for discussions, interviews, and architecture rounds.

## Beginner questions

- What is a token?
- What is a context window?
- How does a language model generate text?
- Why do prompts matter?
- What is the difference between training and inference?

## Intermediate questions

- Why are transformers better than older sequence models for many tasks?
- What are embeddings, and why are they useful?
- What is cosine similarity?
- When should you use RAG instead of fine-tuning?
- What are top-k and top-p sampling?

## Advanced questions

- What are the main failure modes of RAG systems?
- How would you evaluate a GenAI assistant beyond surface-level quality?
- What are the safety risks in tool-using agents?
- How do batching, caching, and KV cache affect inference performance?
- What are the tradeoffs between bigger models and smaller specialized systems?

## System design prompts

- Design a customer-support RAG chatbot.
- Design an internal company knowledge assistant.
- Design a coding copilot with repository retrieval.
- Design a multimodal assistant that can read screenshots and answer questions.
- Design a safe tool-using agent for workflow automation.

## Mastery checklist

You are in strong shape if you can:

- explain tokenization, embeddings, attention, and decoding clearly
- compare prompting, RAG, fine-tuning, and agents appropriately
- write or explain a basic RAG pipeline
- discuss evaluation and guardrails concretely
- reason about latency, cost, and production rollout

## Final advice

The best GenAI interview answers are not just model-centric. Strong candidates connect:

- model behavior
- retrieval or memory design
- product requirements
- reliability and safety
- operational tradeoffs
